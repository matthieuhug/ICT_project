Welcome to the Open-Source 4G/5G Network Implementation Hub

As mobile connectivity continues to drive innovation across industries, implementing 4G and 5G networks has become essential not only for telecommunications but also for research, development, and educational purposes. This site is a dedicated resource for those interested in deploying experimental, open-source 4G and 5G networks—whether you’re a researcher, network engineer, or tech enthusiast looking to explore advanced mobile network solutions.

Our Mission

The goal of this site is to simplify the process of building open-source 4G and 5G networks. With practical guides, expert insights, and a curated selection of resources, we aim to demystify the technical requirements and best practices needed to deploy and optimize open-source cellular networks. By centralizing knowledge, tips, and tools, this platform enables you to accelerate network setup and experimentation, transforming complex technical challenges into achievable tasks.

Who This Site is For

Our platform caters to a diverse audience, including:

  • Researchers: Looking to explore LTE and 5G features, test protocols, and simulate advanced configurations within a controlled environment.
  • Engineers and Technologists: Seeking a cost-effective solution for developing and testing network architectures in experimental, lab, or pilot settings.
  • Students and Educators: Needing an accessible and educational approach to understanding cellular network principles through hands-on, open-source projects.

What You’ll Find Here

This site is organized to support a range of users, from beginners setting up their first LTE network to advanced users experimenting with 5G protocols. Our resources are segmented to facilitate quick access to relevant information:

  • Project Examples: A comprehensive overview of leading open-source projects such as OpenAirInterface (OAI) and srsRAN. Each example includes project backgrounds, capabilities, setup instructions, and real-world applications, enabling you to choose the best platform for your objectives.
  • Step-by-Step Implementation Guides: Our guides cover everything from basic LTE deployment to complex 5G configurations. Organized into easy-to-follow steps, these tutorials provide clear instructions for setting up, optimizing, and troubleshooting network implementations according to your specific needs and resources.
  • Technical Resources & Tools: A carefully selected library of tools and software essentials, covering SDR (Software Defined Radio) options, virtualization software, network testing tools, and protocol libraries. Each resource is vetted to enhance efficiency, compatibility, and reliability in your setup.
  • Best Practices & Optimization Tips: Discover insights into performance tuning, protocol customization, and network security. Our tips draw on industry standards and are designed to ensure your network setup is both robust and adaptable.

Why Open-Source 4G/5G?

Open-source 4G/5G implementations offer unparalleled flexibility and accessibility, enabling users to experiment and innovate without the high cost or constraints of proprietary systems. By leveraging SDR technology and adaptable software architectures, open-source platforms allow for real-time adjustments, modular deployments, and extensive customization—all crucial for experimental and research-oriented networks. Open-source also promotes a community-driven ecosystem where knowledge, code, and solutions are shared, accelerating the development of mobile network technology.

Cost Comparison: Paid 5G Test Benches vs. Open-Source Solutions

While commercial services like AWS and Azure provide quick deployment for 5G networks, they come at a high cost. Open-source tools like OpenAirInterface (OAI) and srsRAN, on the other hand, offer a more flexible, cost-effective solution, especially for research and experimental use. Here's a comparison of the costs associated with paid services versus the benefits of open-source platforms:

Service Name Initial Cost / Fee Key Advantages Why Open-Source is Better
AWS Private 5G $14,400 for 60 days Easy to deploy, cloud-based scalability High cost, limited to 4G, future 5G
Azure Private 5G Variable (fixed throughput pricing) Scalable, secure, and global network management Can be costly at scale; open-source offers flexibility without recurring fees
OpenAirInterface (OAI) Free (open-source) Full 4G/5G network stack, community support No upfront costs, highly customizable
srsRAN Free (open-source) Lightweight, modular, flexible deployment No costs, fully customizable at scale

Why Open-Source is More Cost-Effective: Open-source solutions like OAI and srsRAN are free to use, whereas cloud-based services like AWS and Azure involve ongoing costs based on data usage and network size. Open-source tools allow for greater flexibility in network setups and enable users to scale and experiment without incurring additional charges for each device or data transfer.
